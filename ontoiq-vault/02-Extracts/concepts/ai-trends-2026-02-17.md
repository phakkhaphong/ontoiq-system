## 1. Multi-Agent Systems

**Definition:** AI systems where multiple autonomous agents collaborate to accomplish complex tasks that would be difficult for a single agent.

**Key Points:**
- Agents can communicate and coordinate with each other
- Enables division of labor and parallel processing
- Useful for complex workflows requiring multiple capabilities

**Relevance:** Becoming mainstream in 2026, allowing more sophisticated AI applications.

---

## 2. Tool Use Protocols

**Definition:** Standardized methods that enable AI agents to interact with external tools, APIs, and services to extend their capabilities.

**Key Points:**
- AI can call external functions and services
- Expands beyond training data limitations
- Enables real-time data access and action-taking

**Relevance:** Critical for practical AI applications that need to interact with the real world.

---

## 3. Long-Context Models

**Definition:** AI models with significantly expanded context windows that can process and understand much larger amounts of text in a single interaction.

**Key Points:**
- Can handle entire documents, books, or codebases
- Better understanding of complex relationships
- Reduced need for chunking or summarization

**Relevance:** Enables deeper analysis of complex documents and more coherent long-form content generation.

---

## 4. Reasoning Improvements

**Definition:** Advances in AI architecture that enhance logical reasoning, problem-solving, and step-by-step thinking capabilities.

**Key Points:**
- Better performance on complex reasoning tasks
- Chain-of-thought and similar techniques
- Improved accuracy on math, logic, and planning problems

**Relevance:** Critical for applications requiring reliable decision-making and analysis.

---

## 5. Safety Frameworks

**Definition:** Structured approaches and guidelines for developing and deploying AI systems responsibly and safely.

**Key Points:**
- Guidelines for responsible AI development
- Mechanisms to prevent harmful outputs
- Alignment with human values and intentions

**Relevance:** Increasing priority as AI capabilities grow, ensuring beneficial and controlled AI deployment.

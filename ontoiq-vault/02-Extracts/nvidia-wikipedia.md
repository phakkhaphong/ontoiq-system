## Overview

**Nvidia Corporation** (en-VID-ee-É™) is an American technology company headquartered in Santa Clara, California. Founded in 1993 by Jensen Huang, Chris Malachowsky, and Curtis Priem, it develops graphics processing units (GPUs), systems on chips (SoCs), and application programming interfaces (APIs) for data science, high-performance computing, video games, and mobile and automotive applications. Nvidia has been described as a Big Tech company.

## Key Products and Technologies

### Graphics Processing
- **GeForce GPUs** - Gaming and creative workloads
- **Professional GPUs** - Edge computing, scientific research, industrial applications
- **GeForce Now** - Cloud gaming service

### AI and Data Center
- **CUDA** - Software platform for parallel computing on GPUs
- **AI chips** - Used for training and deploying AI models (80%+ market share as of 2025)
- **Supercomputing** - Chips for over 75% of world's TOP500 supercomputers

### Mobile and Automotive
- **Tegra** - Mobile processors for smartphones, tablets, automotive infotainment
- **Drive PX-series** - AI platform for autonomous vehicles

## History Highlights

### Founding (1993)
Founded in a Denny's roadside diner on Berryessa Road in San Jose. With $40,000 in the bank, the company was born and received $20 million in venture capital funding.

### Key Milestones
- **1997:** RIVA 128 saved the company from bankruptcy
- **1999:** Went public on January 22
- **2000:** Acquired 3dfx intellectual assets
- **2006:** Launched CUDA platform development
- **2019:** Acquired Mellanox Technologies for $6.9 billion
- **2020:** Attempted Arm acquisition (later abandoned due to regulatory challenges)

### Recent Developments
- **2024:** Blackwell AI chip and microarchitecture announced
- **2025:** Released Llama-3.1-Nemotron-Ultra reasoning LLM
- **2025:** Major investment in Intel ($5 billion)
- **2025:** Memorandum of understanding with OpenAI ($100 billion investment)
